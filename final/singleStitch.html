Single Crochet

Single crochet is one of the most common beginner stitches in crochet. It is often learned soon after the chain stitch and is used to create a firm, even fabric for many projects. Because it is simple to repeat and easy to recognize, single crochet is a great stitch for practicing control, tension, and consistency.

Why the Single Crochet Stitch Matters

The single crochet stitchmatters because it is a basic stitch that helps build real crochet fabric. It is shorter and tighter than taller stitches, which makes it useful for projects that need a sturdy texture and good shape. Once you are comfortable with this stitch, you can use it in beginner projects like dishcloths, simple accessories, and plush items, while also building skills that carry over into many other crochet patterns.

Terminology Tip

In UK crochet patterns, the stitch known as single crochet in the US is called double crochet. This difference can be confusing for beginners, so always check whether a pattern uses US or UK terms before starting.

Steps for Making a Single Crochet Stitch

  1. Start with a foundation chain.
  2. Insert your hook into the correct chain or stitch.
  3. Wrap the yarn over the hook.
  4. Pull up a loop so there are now two loops on the hook.
  5. Wrap the yarn over again.
  6. Pull through both loops on the hook.

Beginner Tips

  • Count your stitches often so you do not accidentally add or lose stitches.
  • Keep your tension even so your rows stay neat.
  • Practice slowly until the steps feel natural.
  • Use a light-colored yarn at first so the stitches are easier to see.
